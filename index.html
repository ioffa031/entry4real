3/28/2017

Memory

I keep remembering 45th street With the girl and the red dress Something like a movie With her crimped hair and angel’s lips The car on 34th With its orangey-yellow windows In a color that I can’t seem to get right And a vintage convertible roof The bench on 23rd A blinking street light above Providing just enough light For a passing soul to glance at And feel as if something’s watching them A bluish undertone so it seems almost sad The bridge over 12th With a large curve that puts me out of breath Every time I even see it Where probably hundreds of people have come and gone In a black out The girl on 1st Clicking her red heels in distress Home, home, home To a world with less color More security A grey spot in a primary place.

I keep remembering 45th street
With the girl and the red dress
Something like a movie
With her crimped hair and angel’s lips
The car on 34th
With its orangey-yellow windows
In a color that i can’t seem to get right
And a vintage convertible roof
The bench on 23rd
A blinking street light above
Providing just enough light
For a passing soul to glance at
And feel as if something’s watching them
A bluish undertone so it seems almost sad
The bridge over 12th
With a large curve that puts me out of breath
Every time i even see it
Where probably hundreds of people have come and gone
In a black out
The girl on 1st
Clicking her red heels in distress
Home, home, home
To a world with less color,
more security,
A grey spot in a primary place.
